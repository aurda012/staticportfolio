This Is Me.

My Story

Born in Venezuela, I had a very different experience growing up. Safety was never taken for granted and along with that, neither was my family. These safety concerns eventually pushed my family to move to the United States. I brought my 8 year-old self, my never-ending ambition, and bilingual abilities to Florida.

Ambition was part of me.

I took up coding as a hobby but I decided to make it my career.

As far back as I can recall, I’ve always been a builder and a dreamer. I remember putting together beautiful city architectures out of playblocks in my kindergarten class, while my classmate was busy sucking boogers out of his nose through a bendy-straw. That’s when I knew I was different. As a driven intellectual, I strive by doing and seeing results, by achieving something concrete.


A common trait among gifted students, I started working at a law firm when I was 16 and began taking college classes to meet my high school requirements. My motivation was a car and relief from the stiflingly rigid secondary school system. When I enrolled in college full-time, I found learning by memorization to be pointless. I thrive by doing and seeing results.

Startup Institute

I came across an ad for the Startup Institute one day and it was like a sign from the heavens. I applied, got accepted, packed my bags, and got on a flight from Miami to Boston. During my time here, It’s been a common occurrence for me to catch myself lost in coding through the early A.M.. I’ve managed to go from a self-taught web designer, to having the ability to create complex web applications using Ruby on Rails, Spec, Capybara, Bootstrap, Javascript, React, Redux, Node, and the ever-growing list. Now I’m here, at only 21, pursuing my dreams and excited for my future. My aspiration is to work alongside a startup, creating something that will have a positive impact on society. Let’s build something beautiful together.
